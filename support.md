## Quick Help Guide

### Searching for Foods
- Open the **Search** tab
- Select your preferred database from the dropdown menu
- Type the food name in the search bar
- Tap a food to view details and add to your diary

### Using the Barcode Scanner
- Tap the **camera icon** in the search interface
- Point your camera at the product barcode
- The app will automatically scan and retrieve nutritional information
- Works with Open Food Facts database

### Getting a USDA API Key
- Visit [fdc.nal.usda.gov](https://fdc.nal.usda.gov)
- Sign up for a free account
- Generate an API key
- Enter the key in NutriComposer Settings > USDA API Key
- This unlocks access to Foundation Foods database

### Using the AI Assistant
- **Requirements:** iOS 26+ with Apple Intelligence enabled
- Tap the AI Assistant button on the Dashboard
- **Chat mode:** Type your questions or commands
- **Voice mode:** Tap the microphone to speak
- Ask about nutrients, get food suggestions, log foods by voice

### Enabling Apple Health Integration
- Go to **Settings** > **Apple Health**
- Toggle on "Enable Apple Health"
- Grant permissions in the iOS Health app
- The app will read calories burned and step count

### Setting Up Siri Commands
- Open NutriComposer and go to **Settings**
- Scroll to the **Siri Shortcuts** section
- Tap "Add to Siri" for your preferred commands
- Use commands like "Hey Siri, check nutrients in NutriComposer"

### Creating Custom Foods
- **Method 1:** In the Food Search view, go to the "Custom" tab, then tap the "+" button to create a new custom food
- **Method 2:** Use AI Assistant - say "Create [food name] with [nutrients]"
  - Example: "Create salmon 160g with 166 cal, 26g protein"
- **Method 3:** Copy any existing food and edit it as custom (Copy to Custom feature)

### Tracking Water Intake
- Tap the **water drops icon** on the Dashboard
- Use the + and - buttons to adjust
- Set your daily water goal in Settings

### Importing and Exporting Data
- Go to **Settings** > **Data Management**
- **Export Food Diary:** Save your diary as CSV
- **Import Food Diary:** Load entries from CSV
- **Import Custom Foods:** Bulk import custom foods via CSV

## Troubleshooting

### App Not Finding Foods?
- **Solution:** Try selecting a different database from the dropdown menu
- Open Food Facts: Best for packaged/branded foods
- FNDDS: Survey foods and common items
- Branded Foods: Large database of packaged products
- Foundation Foods: Requires USDA API key

### AI Assistant Not Responding?
- **Check Requirements:** 
  - iOS 26 or later installed
  - Apple Intelligence enabled on your device
  - Check Settings > Apple Intelligence
- **Restart:** Try closing and reopening the app
- **Device Compatibility:** Ensure your iPhone supports Apple Intelligence

### Health Data Not Syncing?
- **Check Permissions:**
  - Open iOS **Settings** > **Health** > **NutriComposer**
  - Verify that calories and steps have read permissions
- **Re-enable:** Toggle off and on Apple Health in NutriComposer Settings
- **Restart:** Restart both the Health app and NutriComposer

### Barcode Scanner Not Working?
- **Camera Permission:** Check Settings > NutriComposer > Camera
- **Lighting:** Ensure good lighting on the barcode
- **Database:** Barcode scanning uses Open Food Facts database
- **Manual Entry:** If scanning fails, manually search for the product

### Food Not in Database?
- **Try Different Database:** Switch databases in the dropdown
- **Create Custom Food:** Add it manually in the Custom tab
- **Contribute:** Consider adding the food to Open Food Facts community database
